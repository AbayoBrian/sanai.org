OUR PROFILE

Who We Are

SANAI is a community-focused organization dedicated to empowering vulnerable populations and addressing socio-economic determinants of health in Africa.

Our work focuses on building resilient livelihoods through integrated development programs that create wealth and promote health in Baringo County and beyond.

We believe in holistic community development that addresses the interconnected challenges of poverty, health, education, and environmental sustainability.

OUR APPROACH

We work with communities to identify their needs and develop sustainable solutions that build local capacity and promote self-reliance. Our programs are designed to create lasting change by addressing root causes rather than symptoms.

MANGO PROJECT DETAILS

Project Background

Barwessa, a semi-arid region in Baringo County, has long faced challenges including land degradation, inter-community conflict, and economic instability. The Pokot, Marakwet, and Tugen communities have historically experienced tensions, primarily over pasture and water resources.

The Baringo Mango Growers Unity Project was formed to unite these communities through a peaceful and productive approach: mango farming. Mango trees provide shade, restore soil fertility, offer annual cash flow, and promote long-term sustainability and food security.

PROJECT GOALS

  • Promote peaceful coexistence between Pokot, Marakwet, and Tugen communities
  • Establish mango farming as a reliable source of annual household income
  • Rehabilitate degraded, dry land with tree cover
  • Empower women and youth with agribusiness skills
  • Enhance food security and resilience to climate change
  • Lay the foundation for future mango value addition and processing

Project Achievements

80 Farmers Supported

10 Marakwet, 20 Pokot, and 50 Tugen members trained and supported

2,560 Mango Trees Planted

Each farmer growing 32 mango trees for sustainable income

Community Unity

Historic tensions reduced through shared agricultural goals

Environmental Restoration

Degraded land rehabilitated with tree cover improving soil fertility

Current Needs

Fencing: Protective fencing needed for 80 farmers at $1,500 per farmer ($120,000 total) to protect young mango seedlings from livestock.

Expansion: Resources needed to onboard 300 more farmers across the three communities.
